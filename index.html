About

Most engineering problems look technical.
They're usually leadership problems in disguise.

My journey began as a software engineer with nearly a decade working on distributed systems, platforms, and cloud-native applications. That foundation is what lets me operate credibly across both the technical and human dimensions.

My job as a leader is to create the conditions where talented people can do the best work of their careers — removing friction, building clarity, and ensuring engineering and business strategy are actually speaking the same language.

I've led teams of 40+ engineers directly and influenced organizations of 800+. Whether it's untangling complexity, rescuing high-stakes initiatives, or scaling systems and teams under pressure, I operate comfortably across both the technical and human dimensions.

Selected impact

  • Revenue growth Drove significant incremental revenue leading cross-functional initiatives that aligned engineering, product, and data science under high-pressure delivery timelines.
  • ML reliability Materially improved ML pipeline reliability, significantly reducing incident load and enabling teams to shift focus from firefighting to model iteration.
  • Efficiency gains Reduced engineering toil by over 50% using KTLO and on-call metrics to build a data-backed investment case, securing headcount and reprioritizing the roadmap.
  • Workflow modernization Transformed the developer workflow via cloud workstations, GitOps, and CI/CD best practices — increasing deployment frequency from bi-weekly to daily.
  • Scale & availability Led a large-scale cloud migration and high-availability rollout for a global brand's core search and product services, meeting an aggressive timeline without disrupting global uptime.

Non-negotiable: build things that genuinely solve customer problems — and be intellectually honest about whether they actually do.
